Terminology

SH%: Percentage of shots that lead to goals. The higher shooting percentage means the more goals you score on fewer shots.
Expected Goals: Expected goals (xG) is a model-based metric used to isolate the evaluation of play-driving and chance-creation/suppression ability from things a player cannot control such as bounces, quality of goaltender, etc.
GSAA: Goals Saved Above Average is the leagueâ€™s average save percentage with the number of shots a goalie has faced. This number is how many goals a league-average goalie would allow.
HDSV%: High Danger Save Percentage is the Save percentage of a goaltender on shots that are classified as a high danger chance.
SV%: Save Percentage is the Save percentage of a goaltender on all shots
